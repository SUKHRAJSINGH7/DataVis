Exploring the Impact of Cost on Higher Education

By Sukhraj Singh

This project explores how the rising cost of higher education in the United States is affecting students and society. Using interactive data visualizations, we examine tuition trends, enrollment shifts, post-graduation debt, and the economic return on different degrees. The goal is to tell a data-driven story about the growing burden of college costs and the limited financial outcomes for many graduates.

Tuition Growth Over Time (All Institutions)

Over the past six decades, the cost of attending college in the United States has increased dramatically. This chart shows a long-term view of tuition growth, revealing a consistent rise that outpaces inflation and wage growth. This rise is the root cause of much of the downstream pressure now seen across society.

Student Debt Over Time (Undergraduate vs Graduate)

With costs rising, students have turned to borrowing. The chart below shows the growth of undergraduate and graduate student debt over the past two decades. Graduate debt is rising especially fast, driven by expensive programs like law, business, and medicine.

Breakdown of Average American Debt

Student loans are now a major part of household debt. This donut chart shows how student debt compares to other forms like mortgages, auto loans, and credit card balances — pushing the average American’s total debt above $100,000.

Cost of Living Index by State (2025)

Debt hits harder depending on where you live. This map shows the cost of living index by state for 2025. In expensive states like California or New York, even high post-grad salaries don’t go as far, while lower-cost states may offer more room — but fewer economic opportunities.

College Enrollment Trends Over Time

The effect of rising tuition is now starting to show up in enrollment rates. For the first time in decades, college enrollment is falling. This drop reflects how many are reconsidering whether the cost of college is worth it at all.
